<p><p>Epidural anesthesia is a type of regional anesthesia that involves injecting local anesthetic drugs into the epidural space, which is the area surrounding the spinal cord in the spinal canal. This form of anesthesia is commonly used during childbirth and surgery to provide pain relief and reduce discomfort.</p><p>The epidural anesthesia system is a medical device designed to administer epidurals safely and accurately. It consists of three main components: an epidural needle, an epidural catheter, and an infusion pump. The needle is used to place the catheter in the epidural space, and the pump delivers the anesthetic drugs continuously or intermittently.</p><p>The global epidural anesthesia system market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 7.40% during the forecast period.
