<p><p>Cord-end ferrules come in two main types: insulated and non-insulated. Insulated ferrules have a plastic sleeve that provides electrical insulation and protection from contact with other conductors, while non-insulated ferrules are simply metal sleeves without any additional insulation. Insulated ferrules are typically used in applications where there is a risk of electrical short circuits, while non-insulated ferrules are more commonly used in applications where electrical insulation is not a concern. Both types play a crucial role in ensuring a secure and reliable electrical connection.</p></p>
