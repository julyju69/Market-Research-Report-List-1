Magnetic phone cards store information on a magnetic strip, which is swiped to activate the card. Optical phone cards use microscopic pits and lands on a reflective surface to store data, and are read using light beams. Chip cards store information on an embedded microchip and require insertion into a compatible device. Remote market phone cards can be accessed remotely, usually via mobile apps, eliminating the need for physical cards.</p></p>
