<p><p>The hip orthoses market consists of two main types of orthotics: dynamic orthotics and static orthotics. Dynamic orthotics are designed to provide continuous and controlled movement assistance to the hip joint. They are typically used for patients with severe hip instability or disability. On the other hand, static orthotics are used to immobilize and support the hip joint, providing stability and reducing pain. These orthotics are commonly recommended for patients recovering from hip fractures, surgeries, or who experience hip dysplasia. Both types of orthotics play a crucial role in supporting and improving hip function for patients with various conditions.</p></p>
