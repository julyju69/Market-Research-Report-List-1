<p><p>In-vitro colorectal cancer screening tests refer to diagnostic tests conducted outside the human body to detect colorectal cancer. The market can be categorized into three types: biomarker tests, fecal occult blood tests (FOBT), and colorectal cancer (CRC) DNA screening tests. Biomarker tests analyze certain substances in blood or tissue samples to identify potential cancer indicators. FOBT examines stool samples for traces of blood. CRC DNA screening tests analyze genetic material in stool samples to detect genetic alterations associated with colorectal cancer. These tests collectively contribute to the early detection and prevention of colorectal cancer.</p></p>
