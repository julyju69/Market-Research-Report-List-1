<p><p>Range extenders, also known as signal boosters or WiFi extenders, are devices designed to expand the coverage area of a wireless network. They work by receiving the existing WiFi signal from a router and then amplifying and retransmitting it to areas with poor signal strength or dead zones. This helps to improve the internet connection and extend the range of the network to reach distant areas.</p><p>The global range extenders market has been experiencing steady growth over the past few years and is expected to continue growing at a CAGR of 4.7% during the forecast period. The increasing adoption of smart devices, rising internet penetration, and growing demand for high-speed internet in residential and commercial sectors are the key drivers contributing to the growth of this market.</p><p>One of the major trends in the range extenders market is the integration of advanced technologies. Manufacturers are focusing on developing range extenders with features like dual-band support, mesh networking, and beamforming technology to ensure better coverage, improved performance, and seamless connectivity. Additionally, the emergence of WiFi 6 technology, which offers faster speeds and enhanced capacity, is expected to drive the demand for range extenders in the coming years.</p><p>Furthermore, the growing demand for smart homes and smart devices, along with the increasing popularity of online gaming and streaming services, is also fueling the market growth. Consumers are increasingly looking for ways to eliminate dead zones and ensure a reliable and robust internet connection throughout their homes or offices.</p><p>Overall, the range extenders market is expected to witness significant growth in the coming years due to the increasing demand for better WiFi coverage, improved internet connectivity, and the proliferation of smart devices.</p></p>

<p><p>Range extenders are devices used to expand the coverage and improve the signal strength of a wireless network. There are three types of range extenders available in the market. </p><p>Single Band (2.4 GHz) Range Extenders operate on a single frequency band, providing a wider coverage area but with limited data transfer speeds. </p><p>Dual Band (2.4 GHz+5 GHz) Range Extenders operate on two frequency bands, offering faster data transfer speeds and better performance in crowded areas. </p><p>Tri-Band (2.4 GHz+5 GHz+5 GHz) Range Extenders operate on three frequency bands, providing even greater performance and reduced interference. These range extenders are ideal for large homes or offices with high network demands.</p></p>
