<p><p>Respiratory antivirals are medications used to treat respiratory viral infections. There are different types of antiviral drugs available on the market. Nucleoside analogs are drugs that inhibit viral replication by interfering with the synthesis of viral DNA or RNA. Neuraminidase inhibitors target the neuraminidase enzyme, preventing the release of new viral particles. Ion channel blockers work by inhibiting the ion channels necessary for viral replication. Fusion protein inhibitors prevent the fusion of viral and host cell membranes, thus inhibiting viral entry into the cells.</p></p>
