<p><p>Radiofrequency (RF) ablation is a minimally invasive procedure used to treat certain medical conditions by using thermal energy to destroy abnormal tissues. Two types of RF ablation systems include temperature-controlled RF ablation, which uses temperature feedback to control the energy delivery, and fluid-cooled RF ablation, which uses a circulating fluid to cool the electrode tip and prevent overheating of surrounding tissues. Both types of RF ablators offer precise and effective treatment options for patients with various medical conditions.</p></p>
