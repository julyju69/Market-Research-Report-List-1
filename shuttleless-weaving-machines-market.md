<p><p>Shuttleless weaving machines have revolutionized the textile industry by eliminating the need for a shuttle to weave fabric. There are three main types of shuttleless weaving machines: air jet, water jet, and rapier and projectile. Air jet machines use compressed air to propel the weft thread, while water jet machines use water streams. Rapier machines utilize a rapier hook to insert the weft, whereas projectile machines have a projectile to insert the weft across the fabric. These different types cater to varying fabric requirements, enabling efficient and high-quality fabric production.</p></p>
