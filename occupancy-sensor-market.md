<p><p>The Occupancy Sensor market includes three primary types: PIR (passive infrared) Sensors, Ultrasonic Sensors, and Dual-technology Occupancy Sensors. PIR Sensors detect infrared energy emitted by people or objects, making them ideal for areas with minimal airflow or obstructed views. Ultrasonic Sensors use sound waves to detect motion, providing comprehensive coverage in large spaces. Dual-technology Occupancy Sensors combine both PIR and Ultrasonic technologies to offer enhanced accuracy and reliability. These sensors play a crucial role in saving energy and increasing convenience by automatically turning lights on or off based on occupancy.</p></p>
