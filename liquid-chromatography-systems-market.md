<p><strong>The Liquid Chromatography Systems Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Liquid-Liquid Chromatography (LLC)</li><li>Liquid-Solid Chromatography (LSC)</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Liquid chromatography (LC) systems are widely used in analytical chemistry for separating and analyzing complex mixtures. There are two main types of LC systems: liquid-liquid chromatography (LLC) and liquid-solid chromatography (LSC). </p><p>In LLC, the separation is based on the differential solubilities of the sample components in two immiscible liquids. This technique is commonly used for separating compounds with similar polarity and is useful for pharmaceutical and environmental analysis.</p><p>In LSC, the separation is based on the interaction between the sample components and a solid stationary phase. This technique is versatile and suitable for a wide range of applications, including drug analysis, environmental monitoring, and food analysis.</p></p>
