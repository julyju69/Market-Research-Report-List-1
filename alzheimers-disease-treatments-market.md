<p><p>The Alzheimer's Disease Treatments market consists of two types of medications: Cholinesterase inhibitors and NMDA antagonists. Cholinesterase inhibitors work by increasing the levels of certain chemicals in the brain that are important for memory and communication between nerve cells. They can help slow down the progression of Alzheimer's symptoms. NMDA antagonists block the activity of a specific receptor in the brain that is involved in learning and memory. They can also improve cognitive function and delay the progression of the disease. Both types of medications have been approved by regulatory authorities for the treatment of Alzheimer's disease.</p></p>
