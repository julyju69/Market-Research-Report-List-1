<p><p>The helicopter engines market consists of three main types: piston engines, gas turbine engines, and stamping engines. Piston engines use reciprocating motion to convert fuel combustion into rotary motion for propelling the helicopter. Gas turbine engines, on the other hand, utilize the energy produced by the combustion of gas fuel to propel the aircraft. Lastly, stamping engines utilize a combination of a piston engine and a gas turbine engine, offering more flexibility and control during flight. These engines serve as the power source for helicopters, enabling their vertical takeoff, hovering, and maneuverability in the air.</p></p>
