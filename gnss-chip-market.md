<p><p>The GNSS chip market consists of two main types: high precision GNSS chips and standard precision GNSS chips. High precision GNSS chips are designed to provide highly accurate positioning information with centimeter-level accuracy. These chips are commonly used in applications that require high accuracy, such as surveying, mapping, and autonomous vehicles. On the other hand, standard precision GNSS chips offer lower accuracy, typically with meter-level accuracy. These chips are suitable for applications like personal navigation devices, fitness trackers, and mobile phones.</p></p>
