<p><p>An artificial kidney, also known as a renal support system, is an advanced medical device designed to perform the functions of a healthy kidney in patients suffering from end-stage renal disease (ESRD). It aims to replicate the vital functions of a kidney, such as removing waste products and excess fluid from the blood, regulating electrolyte levels, and maintaining acid-base balance.  Traditional dialysis machines, although effective, are limited in their capabilities and represent a burdensome treatment for patients. Artificial kidneys aim to offer a more efficient and comfortable alternative.</p><p>The artificial kidney market is expected to witness significant growth during the forecast period. Factors driving this growth include the rising prevalence of chronic kidney diseases, technological advancements in artificial kidney devices, and the increasing demand for efficient and patient-friendly treatment options. Additionally, the growing aging population and the inadequate supply of donor kidneys for transplant have further propelled the demand for artificial kidney devices.</p><p>Moreover, the market has seen the introduction of several innovative products and treatment methods. <p><p>The Artificial Kidney Market includes two types: Wearable Artificial Kidney and Implantable Artificial Kidney. The Wearable Artificial Kidney is a portable device designed for continuous blood filtration in patients suffering from kidney failure. It offers mobility and convenience, allowing patients to maintain their daily activities while receiving treatment. On the other hand, the Implantable Artificial Kidney is a surgically implanted device that mimics the functions of a natural kidney. It provides a more permanent solution for patients with end-stage renal disease, eliminating the need for dialysis and improving their quality of life.</p></p>
