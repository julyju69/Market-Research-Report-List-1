<p><p>Arrayed Waveguide Grating (AWG) is a device used in optical communication systems for multiplexing and demultiplexing wavelengths. The AWG market is categorized into two types: Thermal AWG and Athermal AWG. Thermal AWGs require temperature control to maintain stable device performance, making them suitable for applications where temperature stability is critical. Athermal AWGs, on the other hand, are designed to operate over a wide temperature range without requiring temperature control. Athermal AWGs are preferred for applications where temperature stability is not a major concern, allowing for cost-effective solutions.</p></p>
