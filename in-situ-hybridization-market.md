<p><ul><li>Fluorescence In Situ Hybridization (FISH)</li><li>Chromogenic In Situ Hybridization</li></ul></p>
<p><p>In situ hybridization market types include fluorescence in situ hybridization (FISH) and chromogenic in situ hybridization. FISH is a technique that uses fluorescently labeled DNA probes to detect and localize specific DNA sequences in cells, tissues, or chromosomes. It is widely used in cancer diagnosis and genetic research. On the other hand, chromogenic in situ hybridization is a method that employs enzymatic reactions to produce a colored reaction product, allowing for the visualization and detection of specific DNA or RNA sequences. Both techniques serve as valuable tools in molecular biology and diagnostics.</p></p>
