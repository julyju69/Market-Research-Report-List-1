<p><p>Retinal disease therapeutics refer to the drugs and therapies used for the treatment of various retinal diseases, such as age-related macular degeneration, diabetic retinopathy, retinal vein occlusion, and others. The increasing prevalence of retinal diseases, particularly age-related macular degeneration and diabetic retinopathy, is one of the primary drivers. According to the World Health Organization, around 1.7 million people worldwide suffer from age-related macular degeneration, and this number is expected to increase significantly in the coming years. The development of innovative drugs, such as anti-vascular endothelial growth factor (anti-VEGF) inhibitors, has revolutionized the treatment of retinal diseases. These therapies help in reducing abnormal blood vessel growth and fluid leakage, thereby preserving or improving vision.</p><p>The retinal disease therapeutics market is also witnessing favorable reimbursement policies and initiatives by governments and healthcare organizations to improve access to treatment.
