<p><ul><li>Primary Implant</li><li>Partial or Unicompartmental Implant</li><li>Revision Implant</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The hip and knee implant market consists of three main types: primary implants, partial or unicompartmental implants, and revision implants. Primary implants are used in patients with severe joint damage and involve replacing the entire joint with an artificial implant. Partial or unicompartmental implants are used when only one part of the joint is damaged and involve replacing that specific part. Revision implants are used when previous implants fail or wear out and require a replacement. These different types of implants cater to varying degrees of joint damage and provide treatment options for patients accordingly.</p></p>
