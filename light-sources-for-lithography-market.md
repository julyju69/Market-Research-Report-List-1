<p><p>The light sources used in lithography can be classified into two main types: Deep Ultraviolet (DUV) and Extreme Ultraviolet (EUV) light sources. DUV light sources include ArF (Argon Fluoride), KrF (Krypton Fluoride), and i-line (mid-UV wavelength) sources. ArF and KrF light sources are commonly used in the semiconductor industry for producing fine patterns on silicon wafers. EUV light sources, on the other hand, utilize much shorter wavelengths and are emerging as a promising alternative for advanced lithography, offering higher resolution and enabling the production of smaller and more complex semiconductor devices.</p></p>
