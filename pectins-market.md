<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><p>Pectins are types of soluble dietary fibers used in various food applications for their gelling, thickening, and stabilizing properties. High Methoxyl (HM) Pectin is primarily used in making jams, jellies, and fruit fillings due to its ability to form a gel in high-sugar environments. Low Methoxyl (LMC) Pectin, which requires calcium for gel formation, is suitable for products like dairy desserts and acidified beverages. Amidated Low Methoxyl (LMA) Pectin, modified with ammonia, forms a stable gel at low pH levels and is commonly used in dairy products, bakery fillings, and sauces.</p></p>
