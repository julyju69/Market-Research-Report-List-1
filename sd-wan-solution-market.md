<p><p>SD-WAN (Software Defined Wide Area Networking) solutions are categorized into three market types: virtual appliance, physical appliance, and hybrid appliance. </p><p>A virtual appliance is a software-based solution that runs on a virtual machine, allowing organizations to deploy SD-WAN on existing virtualized infrastructure. </p><p>A physical appliance refers to a hardware device that is purpose-built to provide SD-WAN functionality. This appliance is typically deployed as a standalone device or in combination with other networking devices. </p><p>A hybrid appliance combines both virtual and physical components, providing flexibility and scalability in deployment options. It offers the benefits of both virtual and physical appliances to cater to different network requirements.</p></p>
